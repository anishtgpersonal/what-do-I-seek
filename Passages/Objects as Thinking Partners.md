# Objects as Thinking Partners

> We don't become more intelligent solely by improving our brains—we become more intelligent by designing better environments, better tools, and better objects to think with.

Inspired by Andy Clark's extended mind thesis.

Thinking doesn't always stop at the skull. A notebook, a whiteboard, a diagram, or a motorcycle can become part of cognition by changing what we notice, what possibilities occur to us, and how much mental effort we expend.

Humans often mark psychological transitions through physical objects. The object is not merely owned; it becomes an anchor for a new identity and a different way of seeing the world.

A good object is not just something we use. It quietly teaches us how to think and who we are becoming.
